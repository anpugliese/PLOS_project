Sustainable urbanization and walkability

Making cities sustainable means creating career and business opportunities, safe and affordable housing, and building resilient societies and economies. It involves enhancing urban planning and management in participatory and inclusive ways, as well as investing in public transport, creating green public spaces and improving pedestrian mobility.

Pedestrian mobility in urban areas plays a central role in the sustainable development of cities by reducing traffic and air pollution. Well-planed cities should provide safe and comfortable pedestrian infrastructure in such a way that more people will walk, instead of driving, with a positive impact on both the environment and the health and overall quality of their lives.

56% of World’s population live in cities

Today 4.4 billion people live in urban areas, corresponding to 56% of the world’s population. By 2050, urban areas are projected to house 68% of the global population, with one in every three people that will live in cities of at least half a million inhabitants.

Growth in the urban population is driven by overall population increase and by the upward shift in the percentage living in urban areas. Together, these two factors are projected to add 2.3 billion to the world’s urban population by 2050, with almost 90% of this growth happening in Asia and Africa.
